Mercedes-Benz Key Types We Program

Standard Remote Key

Older Mercedes models through roughly 2015 use a remote fob with a separate metal blade. Unlock and lock buttons on the fob, blade inserts in the ignition. Common on older C-Class, E-Class, GLK, and ML.

Locksmith: $180 to $280 typical

Keyless-Go Smart Key

Found on most Mercedes from 2016 onward including C300, E350, GLE 350 and 450, GLS, and AMG variants. The car senses the fob within one to three feet. Touch the door handle to unlock, press the start button to drive. No blade insertion needed.

Locksmith: $280 to $450 typical

Valet Key

A limited-function key that opens the door but does not access the trunk or glove box. Some owners need a replacement after losing the valet key while the main smart key is intact.

Locksmith: $150 to $220 typical

All-Keys-Lost Reset

When every key has been lost, the car's NEC control unit must be cleared and a fresh key paired. Takes longer and costs more than a spare key addition, but fully possible on-site for most models.

Mercedes-Benz Key Questions

How much does a Mercedes-Benz key replacement cost in Westchester?

With us, a Mercedes smart key replacement including cut and programming runs $280 to $450 depending on the model and year. The dealer charges $450 to $700 for the same key plus a separate programming fee of $150 to $200 on top. Call us with your year and model and we quote exactly.

What is Keyless-Go and can you program it on-site?

Keyless-Go is Mercedes' proximity key system used on most C-Class, E-Class, GLE, GLS, and S-Class vehicles from 2016 onward. The car detects the fob in your pocket or bag and unlocks when you touch the door handle. Push-button start works the same way. Yes, we program Keyless-Go keys on-site.

My Mercedes key suddenly stopped being detected. What happened?

Usually a depleted key battery, though a failed proximity antenna or software pairing issue can also cause it. If the backup blade hidden inside the fob can still open the door manually, the fob battery is most likely the cause. If even the backup blade key has been lost or the car electronics are not responding, call us and we diagnose on arrival.

Can you make a key for my Mercedes if I have no working key at all?

Yes. Lost-all-keys situations for Mercedes require clearing the existing key pairings from the car's control module and programming a fresh key. It is possible on-site for most C-Class, E-Class, GLE, GLS, and similar models. Call us with your VIN so we confirm the exact procedure before dispatch.
