Why Park Slope Residents Call Alliance

The Parking Situation in Park Slope

Park Slope has a reputation as a walkable neighborhood, but it still has a massive number of parked cars. Street parking on 5th Avenue and 7th Avenue is brutally competitive on weekend nights, and the residential blocks off Union Street, Garfield Place, and 2nd Street are where people end up parking after circling for 20 minutes. When you finally find a spot and then lock your keys inside, you are not looking for someone to show up "sometime tonight." The Prospect Park parking lot off Flatbush Avenue is another consistent location, particularly in spring and summer when families come in from surrounding neighborhoods and the lot gets chaotic.

The Barclays and BAM Corridor

The Atlantic Avenue and Flatbush intersection is one of the busiest corners in Brooklyn. Barclays Center events drive thousands of people into the area for parking on the side streets between Atlantic Ave and Pacific Street, and on the blocks east toward Dean Street and Bergen Street. Events let out at 10pm, 11pm, midnight, and people return to their cars exhausted and rushed. That is the scenario where keys get locked in. BAM, the Brooklyn Academy of Music on Lafayette Avenue, generates the same pattern on nights when there is a film, opera, or performance. We know the area and we do not need you to describe it in detail, just give us your cross street.
