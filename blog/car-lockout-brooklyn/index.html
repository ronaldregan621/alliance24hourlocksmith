You locked your keys in the car in Brooklyn. Maybe it was the Whole Foods parking lot on Third Street in Park Slope. Maybe it was a side street in Williamsburg after dinner on Bedford Avenue. Do This First (Before You Call Anyone)

Before assuming you need a locksmith, run through this quick check:

  • Check all four doors and the trunk. It sounds obvious, but one in ten people calling for a lockout has an unlocked door they did not try.
  • Check your fob battery. If your push-start car will not open remotely, try holding the fob against the door handle or the start button. Many vehicles have a backup proximity reader there.
  • Check if you have OnStar, FordPass, or a brand app. Some vehicles can be unlocked remotely through the manufacturer's app on your phone.
  • Spare key? If someone nearby has your spare, getting it is faster than waiting for a locksmith. But do not wait more than 20 minutes for a spare if the person is not close.

If none of those work, call a locksmith. Do not try to slim jim or wedge the door yourself. Modern vehicles have anti-slim jim protections and you are likely to damage the weather stripping or trigger the alarm without getting in.

While You Wait

  • Stay with your vehicle. Do not walk away and leave it unattended, especially with your keys visible inside.
  • If it is late at night and you are uncomfortable with your surroundings, wait inside a nearby business or call a friend to keep you company until we arrive.
  • Have your driver's license and vehicle registration ready. We verify ownership before opening any vehicle.
  • If you have children or pets locked inside and cannot wait, call 911 immediately. This is an emergency that law enforcement can assist with faster.
