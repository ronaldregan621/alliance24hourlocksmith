Emergency Locksmith in Queens, NY: What to Know Before You Call

Published 2026-05-22  •  Alliance 24hr Locksmith Editorial

Queens is the most geographically diverse borough -- from Astoria's dense apartment blocks to Far Rockaway's beachside streets. That geographic spread means ETAs vary widely, and it means scam locksmiths have found fertile ground here. This guide tells you what you need to know before you call.

The Queens Locksmith Scam Problem

Queens has a higher concentration of bait-and-switch locksmith operations than any other borough. They advertise "$15 service calls" in local Facebook groups and Google Maps, dispatch technicians who arrive with no ID or uniform, and produce invoices of $300-600 for simple lockouts when you're already outside your car in the cold.

Red flags for scam locksmiths in Queens:
  • "$15 service call" ads -- this price exists nowhere in reality
  • Driver arrives in an unmarked personal vehicle
  • Cannot provide a written or verbal total price before starting work
  • Demands Zelle, Cash App, or cash only on arrival
  • Claims the lock needs to be drilled when it clearly doesn't
