Step by Step: What Happens When You Call

Step 1: The Call (about 3 to 5 minutes)

When you dial (914) 406-4474, a real person picks up. Not a call center, not a voicemail. You give us three things: your location, your vehicle year/make/model, and your phone number. We give you two things back: a firm price and an honest ETA.

The price is not a "starting from" price. It is the price. If something changes once we arrive (a rare situation, usually involving a damaged lock or unusual vehicle), we tell you before doing any additional work. You can confirm or decline.

We do not dispatch until you agree to the price. That is the whole first call: confirm location, confirm price, confirm ETA, and we head your way.

Step 3: The Technician Arrives and Confirms

When the technician arrives, they do two things before touching anything. First, they confirm the vehicle matches what you described on the phone. Second, they ask to see your ID and verify it against the vehicle registration. This step protects you as much as it protects the vehicle owner: a legitimate locksmith should always verify that you have a right to be in the car before opening it.

If you are driving a rental, have the rental agreement ready. If it is a company vehicle, a business card or employee ID that matches the registration company helps.

Step 4: Opening the Car (5 to 15 minutes)

The actual opening is faster than most people expect. For most modern vehicles, the tools are slim wedge devices and a long-reach tool that manipulates the door handle or unlock button through a small gap. The car is opened without any damage to the frame, window, or door seals.

Some vehicles are harder than others. Certain Honda, Hyundai, and late-model Ford models have tighter door gaps that require more care. A skilled technician still gets in without damage, it just takes a bit longer. Anything over 20 minutes on-site is unusual.

Step 5: Payment and You Are On Your Way

We accept Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover. We do not accept cash only, and we do not use Venmo or Zelle. You get a receipt. Total time from call to driving away: typically 45 to 65 minutes in Yonkers and 40 to 60 minutes in White Plains.
