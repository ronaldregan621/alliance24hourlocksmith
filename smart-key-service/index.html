How Smart Keys Work and Why They Are Hard to Duplicate

PROXIMITY SENSING

It is always talking to your car

A smart key broadcasts a low-frequency signal constantly when within range. The car's antenna modules (usually one per door handle) listen for it. When you grab the door handle, the system confirms the fob's rolling code, verifies it against the body control module, and unlocks. No button press needed.

ROLLING CODES

Why you cannot just copy a smart key

Unlike a basic remote, smart keys use rolling code encryption. Every press or use generates a new code pair, and the car and fob stay synchronized. A copy of the fob hardware without the enrolled credentials is worthless. The new fob must be introduced to the vehicle's computer using diagnostic equipment so both sides learn each other's cryptographic credentials.

BACKUP KEY

There is always a blade inside

Every smart key contains a mechanical blade that slides out. It opens the door manually if the fob battery dies completely. There is also usually a backup proximity reader near the start button. If your fob battery is dead, hold it directly against the start button and try. If your car still will not start, you need programming help.

OBD2 PROGRAMMING

What we do that the hardware store cannot

We connect to your vehicle's OBD2 port with professional-grade equipment and write the new key's credentials into the vehicle's body control module. On some makes we also use a dedicated key learning mode. The result is a fully enrolled fob that the car trusts as its own. This is the same process the dealer uses, done at your location.

Common Smart Key Problems We Solve

Push button does not start the car

Try holding the fob directly on the start button (backup reader). If that fails, the fob may have lost pairing or the battery may be fully dead. Replace the CR2032 or CR2025 battery first. If a new battery does not fix it, we can re-enroll the fob on-site.

Door handle does not detect the fob

The exterior door antenna may have failed, or the fob is out of range battery. Start with a fresh battery. If proximity unlocking still does not work but the start button works, it is likely a door antenna issue, not the fob itself.

Key not detected message on dash

This message appears when the car senses a fob is present but cannot authenticate it. Common causes: dead battery, wet fob internals, or de-paired credential. We re-enroll the fob on-site in most cases without replacing the hardware.

Smart Key Questions

What is a smart key?

A smart key communicates with your car wirelessly via low-frequency radio. You keep it in your pocket or bag. The car senses it when you approach, unlocks the door on touch, and allows push-button starting without inserting a key. Smart keys became common from 2005 onward and are now standard on most new vehicles.

Can you program a smart key on-site without a dealer?

Yes, for most makes. We use OBD2-connected programming equipment to enroll new smart key credentials directly to the vehicle's body control module. Some very new models may require a dealer-exclusive security token. We confirm on the phone before dispatch whether your specific vehicle is serviceable on-site.

My push-start car will not start. Is it the key fob?

It may be the fob battery or a fob that lost its pairing. Try holding the fob directly against the start button, many vehicles have a backup proximity reader there. If it still will not start, call us. We can diagnose on-site whether it is the fob, the antenna, or the control module.
